## Major corrections

- In this course, `N = {0,1,2,3,...}`, and infinity is not an element.
- `-17 ∈ Z`, while `14/7 = 2 ∈ N` and `√49 = 7 ∈ N` after simplification.
- A counterexample must satisfy the premise and violate the conclusion.
- `x ∈ A` is element-to-set; `A ⊆ B` is set-to-set.
- `4 + 0i = 4 ∈ R`.
- Population change is naturally integer-valued.
- Frequencies and proportions belong to `[0,1]`.
- A general Fourier coefficient may be complex-valued.
- Closure under division always excludes division by zero.
